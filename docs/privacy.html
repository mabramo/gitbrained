Gitbrained

Privacy Policy — Last updated April 7, 2026

The short version

Gitbrained does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to its developer. Your notes stay on your device and in the git repository you configure. That's it.

What the app does

Gitbrained is a markdown note-taking app that reads and writes files to a git repository you own and control. The app:

  • Stores notes locally on your device
  • Syncs notes to and from a git host of your choice (GitHub, Gitea, Forgejo, or any compatible service) using credentials you provide
  • Stores your personal access token locally in your device's secure storage (Android Keystore)

Data we do not collect

We do not collect, and have no access to:

  • Your notes or their content
  • Your personal access token or any credentials
  • Your name, email address, or any identifying information
  • Usage data, analytics, or crash reports
  • Device identifiers
  • Location data

Third-party services

When you sync your notes, the app communicates directly with the git host you configure (e.g. GitHub). Your use of that service is governed by their own privacy policy. Gitbrained has no involvement in or visibility into that communication beyond initiating it on your behalf.

The app contains no advertising SDKs, analytics frameworks, or crash reporting tools.

Data storage

Your notes are stored in two places, both under your control:

  • Your device — in the app's private local storage
  • Your git repository — the remote you configure in settings

Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data.

Open source

Gitbrained is fully open source. You can inspect exactly what the app does at any time: github.com/mabramo/gitbrained

Contact

Questions about this policy: open an issue on GitHub.

Gitbrained is developed by detached.head. This policy applies to the Android app published on Google Play and F-Droid.
